Bela Nikitina
Member of the Council of the “Re-Haifa” movement, environmental sociologist, PhD

Areas of specialization: environmental governance, circular economy, municipal waste management, resource policy, public participation, public sociology, applied research, and program evaluation.

Professional experience: more than 25 years of research, teaching, and analytical work at the intersection of academia and public practice. Bela Nikitina is a Research Fellow at the Center for Natural Resources and Environmental Management Research at the University of Haifa. She has conducted more than 50 applied studies and is the author of more than 70 academic and expert publications, including monographs and educational textbooks.

Experience working with organizations: she has collaborated with Russian and European universities, research centers, regional governance bodies, and civil society organizations in the Samara region of the Russian Federation, and has participated in projects supported by the MacArthur Foundation, IREX, and the Russian Foundation for Basic Research.

Anna Ziv
Member of the Council of the “Re-Haifa” movement, educational organizer, eco-activist

Areas of specialization: environmental education, sustainable consumption, organization and facilitation of environmental events, development of urban communities, cross-sector collaboration, and management of volunteer projects.

Professional experience: founder and coordinator of the GreenMove initiative promoting an environmentally friendly lifestyle. She organizes and conducts workshops, separate waste collection campaigns, and educational events. She actively uses social media to promote environmental issues.

Experience working with organizations: she has collaborated with municipal institutions and educational organizations. She has worked with NGOs and communities within programs of environmental education and public engagement.

Tatyana Frumina
Member of the Council of the “Re-Haifa” movement, logistician, social activist

Experience in Russia: social activism, development of local communities, accessible urban environment, inclusive infrastructure, sustainable lifestyle, practices of reusing items, urban environmental initiatives, public self-organization.

Experience working with organizations: In Russia, Tatyana worked on issues of transport accessibility and urban infrastructure for people with limited mobility, interacted with municipal authorities, and participated in the development of a local community of more than 1,000 participants. In Israel, she continued her public activity after completing a social activism course in Haifa and becoming involved in environmental roundtables and urban initiatives. Among the areas of her practical work are the organization of Swap Market and other public formats that strengthen a culture of sustainable consumption and horizontal mutual support.

Alexandra Zeitlina
Member of the Council of the “Re-Haifa” movement, project manager, eco-activist

Areas of specialization: bioecology, environmental education, sustainable development, inclusive conservation design, public monitoring, environmental analytics, and data visualization.

Professional experience: She has worked in the field of nature conservation for more than 15 years. She coordinated WWF and Kolarctic CBC projects, as well as a number of initiatives related to biodiversity conservation, algae research, and sustainable tourism development. She has expertise in environmental reporting, communication with NGOs, and popularizing scientific data for a broad audience.

Experience working with organizations: She collaborated with Rosprirodnadzor, the Ministry of Natural Resources of the Murmansk Region, nature reserves, museums, and NGOs (Russia).
